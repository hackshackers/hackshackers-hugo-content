Greetings, hacks and hackers! In case you missed it, the next MisinfoCon will [take place next month](https://misinfocon.com/misinfocon-4-0-washington-dc-1b6ed42c5514) in Washington, D.C.

[Sign up soon](https://www.eventbrite.com/e/misinfocon-dc-a-policy-summit-on-misinformation-registration-46774656256) if you plan to go. Meanwhile, there's plenty afoot this week in Zurich, Vienna, Brisbane and Dar es Salaam.

**Happening this week:**

* Brisbane is learning [data journalism](https://www.meetup.com/Hacks-Hackers-Brisbane/events/250254086/)
* [Miami](http://www.meetup.com/Hacks-Hackers-Miami/) and [IRE](http://www.meetup.com/hackshackersIRE/) are holding their regular open labs
* Zurich is hosting [Alberto Cairo](https://www.meetup.com/Hacks-Hackers-Zurich/events/247732488/)
* Lagos and Abuja are [hosting meetups](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/1YM2sXg18otgVDMJpBiZFjXs-mJkBP1NITyauLl5ZooA/viewform?edit_requested=true)
* Vienna is hosting speakers and [lightning talks](https://www.meetup.com/Hacks-Hackers-Vienna/events/251515634/)
* Dar es Salaam is chatting about [chat bots](https://www.facebook.com/events/1682173465163679/)

**Worth a read:**

* The World Cup is happening, and [nearly half the audience](http://www.niemanlab.org/2018/06/for-the-world-cup-livestreamed-online-video-is-threatening-to-score-the-equalizer-on-traditional-tv/) is watching on livestream rather than television, an advertising study found (NiemanLab) 
* Filter bubbles are not, in fact, [proven to affect large portions of the population](https://medium.com/trust-media-and-democracy/nine-takeaways-from-knight-supported-research-on-restoring-trust-in-news-c3d29ae0de3d) - and other findings from the Knight Foundation white papers on misinformation (Knight)
* Regional markets have suffered in Latin America like they have in much of the world, and [digital native sites are stepping in](https://knightcenter.utexas.edu/blog/00-19856-digital-native-journalism-sites-latin-america-bet-regional-journalism) (Knight Center)
